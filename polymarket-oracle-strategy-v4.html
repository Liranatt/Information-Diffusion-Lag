1. Core idea02 / 10
Information diffusion lag

The bet is not on Polymarket. The bet is on delayed equity repricing.

Prediction markets aggregate event information quickly. Broad ETFs and index arbitrage react first; fundamentally exposed single names can reprice more slowly.

T0

Event appears on Polymarket and enters the monitored universe.

Tθ

Probability crosses the confidence threshold. A candidate can become tradable.

Te - 1

Position exits before binary event resolution to avoid announcement volatility.

Signal source

Polymarket probability is treated as an upstream oracle, not as a trading venue.

Economic target

U.S.-listed equities with a plausible causal exposure to the event outcome.

Execution edge

Capture the anticipatory run-up before the event resolves, then step aside.

2. Data sources and cleaning03 / 10
Point-in-time candidate construction

Every candidate is built only after a live event clears the oracle gate.

Sources

Polymarket Gamma API: active markets, tags, probability paths, market timing.

Gemini router: relevance filter, causal channel, exposed tickers, connection strength.

Interactive Brokers / price data: tradable U.S. symbols, daily bars, benchmark paths.

Cleaning and guards

Discard markets with relevance below 0.50 or invalid IB symbols.

Use T0, Tθ, and Te to separate entry eligibility from training-label eligibility.

Clip price and probability paths at each evaluation boundary to prevent future-path leakage.

Feature families

Polymarket state, time-to-resolution, connection strength, recent asset and benchmark trends, ATR, firm descriptors, event archetype, and sector context.

4. T1 objective05 / 10
Why T1 is different

T1 adds cost awareness directly to the optimizer's fitness.

Interpretation

This is not a live pre-entry hurdle. It is a CEM fitness pressure: prefer policies whose realized trades clear friction by enough to matter.

Friction failure

A trade fails the T1 hurdle when gross P&L is less than 3x its modeled transaction cost.

score = Sharpe_daily - 0.30 * abs(max_drawdown) - 2.00 * friction_fail_rate friction_fail_rate = mean(gross_pnl < 3 * txn_cost)

5. Trade logic and costs06 / 10
From event to position

A trade must survive probability, price-run-up, cost, and portfolio constraints.

Entry

Enter immediately above enter_strong, or require persistence above enter_floor for the optimized hold window.

Pre-entry rejection

Reject entries after excessive probability surge or if the asset already ran too far.

Exit

Exit on probability invalidation, ATR/profit-lock where applicable, or the Te - 1 boundary.

Portfolio

Target 13.7% per position, cap at 9 concurrent positions, and value the portfolio daily.

Cost realism

Each trade is modeled as four funded legs: sell benchmark, buy asset, sell asset, rebuy benchmark. Costs include IB-style commission, sell-side SEC fee, and 5 bp slippage.

6. Backtesting design07 / 10

True portfolio NAV

What changed

T1 pressures CEM toward policies whose winners are large enough to survive real execution costs. T2 and T3 are useful safeguards, but in this OOS run they did not improve terminal SPY performance.

Benchmark caveat

On QQQ, T1 is also the best variant, but it still trails QQQ buy-and-hold. The submitted result should therefore be framed as the SPY-optimized T1 win, not a universal benchmark victory.

9. Interpretation10 / 10
What the result establishes

T1 passes an initial leakage-safe OOS test. The remaining bottleneck is causal mapping.

Validated

A prediction-market threshold can be converted into a cost-aware equity portfolio that outperformed SPY in this 13-month OOS window.

Not proven yet

The result does not prove stable long-run alpha. It needs more independent event windows and pre-registered re-evaluation.

Main weakness

Correct event forecasts do not always become profitable equity trades. Asset selection, timing, and question-to-stock causality remain the hard part.

Final message

Polymarket supplies a high-conviction event oracle. T1 turns it into an executable strategy by making CEM optimize for daily-equity Sharpe after drawdown and friction penalties.
